Relevance is strong on every fixture — selections are on-theme, concrete, and the
shortReasons are warm and non-shaming. No invalid/duplicate IDs, no quotation
failures, no malformed output in any successful run; validation and the crisis
filter held. Representative good picks:

- **anger** → Dhp 222 (hold back rising anger "like a rolling chariot"), 232 (tongue), 103 (conquer self). Empowering, not shaming.
- **conflict** → Dhp 133 (don't speak harshly), 408 (true speech free from harshness), 223 (overcome anger by love). Excellent.
- **rumination / restlessness** → Dhp 35/33/36/25 (taming the flighty mind; "an island no flood can overwhelm"). Gentle, apt.
- **craving** → Dhp 216/336/334 (greed→grief; thirst falls away "like water off a lotus leaf"). On-target.
- **gratitude** → Dhp 194/219/98 (affirming, community/warmth). Good positive-state match.
- **shame [crisis]** → filter pushed it from abstract impermanence verses to gentle mind-taming (Dhp 35/33/39). The crisis filter visibly improved tone here.

## Flags for the human confirmation pass (tasks 2.8 follow-up / 4.9)

1. **Grief under crisis surfaces heavy body/impermanence verses.** With
   `crisis_flag=True`, grief returned Dhp 374 ("origin and destruction of the
   elements of the body"), 279 ("all forms are unreal"), and **203 ("the body the
   greatest of pains")**. These pass the categorical filter (contemplative tone,
   not themed `death`, no `fresh-grief` avoidWhen) but read abstract/heavy for
   someone in acute fresh grief. **Recommendation:** during the human pass,
   consider adding `avoidWhen: fresh-grief` and/or `excludeOnCrisis: true` to the
   most body/mortality-adjacent contemplation verses (203 the strongest candidate;
   review 374, 279, 113, 114). This is precisely the gap `excludeOnCrisis` exists for.

2. **Speech-regret leans mildly admonishing.** Non-crisis speech-regret returned
   Dhp 314 ("an evil deed... a man repents of it afterwards") and 67 ("that deed is
   not well done... the reward of which he receives crying and with a tearful
   face"). Not harmful, and kimi spun them forward-looking, but the verses
   themselves are moral-consequence framed and a touch on-the-nose for someone
   already regretful. **Recommendation:** consider whether `speech-regret` should
   boost reconciliation/non-hatred verses (e.g. 223) over consequence verses, or
   tag 67/314 to down-rank for already-remorseful states. Low priority.

## Mechanical finding (informs runtime config, not the catalog)

`kimi-k2p6` is a **reasoning model**: without constrained decoding it emits
chain-of-thought prose first and overruns the token budget before producing JSON —
every call failed to parse until `response_format: {"type":"json_object"}` was set.
This confirms adapter-plan.md §4.1: **the runtime selector should request
constrained JSON decoding where the provider supports it.** Gemini Flash (the
runtime default) is not a reasoning model so the failure mode is less acute, but the
JSON-mode switch should still be used. The adapter's brace-matched `_extract_json`
is the correct fallback for providers without a JSON mode.
